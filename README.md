# Impact of Survey Non-Uniformity on Galaxy Cluster Peaks in Shear Statistics in DP2

This project will produce weak-lensing mass maps and shear-peak maps, with null tests, from DESC Data Preview 2 (DP2) coadds to analyze CL-scale WL systematics. The goal is to test how robust shear-peak and mass-mapping measurements are in the presence of non-uniform survey conditions. We will correlate peaks and mass-map statistics with survey property maps (such as depth, seeing, sky background, PSF quality, stellar density, masking, airmass, and extinction). We will also examine high SNR peaks in detail to identify possible systematics. For each peak we will check local survey conditions, the shear pattern (including B-mode null tests), and indicators of blending or imaging artifacts such as bright-star halos and edge effects. Peaks will be cross-matched with known cluster catalogs from optical, X-ray, and SZ catalogs, to estimate what fraction corresponds to real clusters. This project focuses on identifying and understanding systematics rather than performing cosmological inference. To keep the analysis simple and deliverable, we will adopt a single mass-mapping estimator, one peak selection function, a conservative source sample with simple photo-z cut, and a strict survey mask. The project will deliver reproducible pipelines for future analyses, mass maps, peak catalogs, validation plots, and a corresponding paper.
